MSc Urban Spatial Science, University College London

Spatial Analysis - CASA0005 Geographic Information Systems and Science

TfL Vision Zero

This is a spatial hotspot analysis examining where deaths and serious injuries cluster across London in 2024 using casualty data provided by TfL. The analysis applied point pattern analysis methods and spatial autocorrelation methods to identify statistically significant hotspot and coldspot areas and discuss policy implications for Vision Zero.

Full report coming soon.

New York Evictions

This is a spatial hotspot analysis examining where eviction activities cluster across New York in 2020 using evictions data provided by NYC Open Data. The analysis applied point pattern analysis methods and spatial autocorrelation methods to identify statistically significant hotspot and coldspot areas, discussing areas to be prioritiesed for policy interventions.

CASA0007 Quantitative Methods

The Geography of Everyday Gambling Exposure in London

This project examines how betting shops are distributed across London neighbourhoods and whether exposure is linked to deprivation, housing pressure and labour-market vulnerability. It combines spatial joining, neighbourhood-level exposure metrics, regression modelling and risk mapping.

View the project page: Gambling Exposure in London

CASA0006 Data Science for Spatial Systems

Spatial Variation in Road Casualty Risk Across London MSOAs

This project investigates spatial inequality in road casualty risk across London neighbourhoods. It combines casualty records, MSOA boundaries, population data and deprivation indicators with spatial autocorrelation, LISA clustering, OLS and Random Forest modelling.

View the project page: London Road Casualty Risk

CEGE0042 Spatial-Temporal Data Analysis and Data Mining

Spatio-Temporal Analysis and Prediction of Urban Crime in Chicago

This project analyses Chicago crime incidents from 2022 to 2024 using exploratory spatial-temporal analysis, spatial autocorrelation, hotspot mapping and predictive modelling. It compares naive, ARIMA, SARIMA and Random Forest approaches for short-term crime forecasting.

View the project page: Chicago Crime STDM Project

BSc Urban Planning and Development, Cardiff University

Urban Regeneration Dissertation

My dissertation compared urban regeneration in Cardiff Bay, UK and Durban Port, South Africa, examining how waterfront regeneration approaches differ across planning, governance and socio-economic contexts.

CASA0007 Quantitative Methods

The Geography of Everyday Gambling Exposure in London

该项目分析伦敦博彩店在社区尺度上的分布，并探讨博彩暴露是否与贫困、住房压力和劳动力市场脆弱性相关。项目结合空间连接、社区暴露指标、回归建模与风险制图。

查看项目页面：伦敦博彩暴露

CASA0006 Data Science for Spatial Systems

Spatial Variation in Road Casualty Risk Across London MSOAs

该项目研究伦敦不同社区道路伤亡风险的空间不平等。项目结合道路伤亡记录、MSOA 边界、人口与贫困指标，并应用空间自相关、LISA 聚类、OLS 与随机森林模型。

查看项目页面：伦敦道路伤亡风险

CEGE0042 Spatial-Temporal Data Analysis and Data Mining

Spatio-Temporal Analysis and Prediction of Urban Crime in Chicago

该项目分析 2022 至 2024 年芝加哥犯罪事件，结合探索性时空分析、空间自相关、热点制图与预测模型，并比较朴素基线、ARIMA、SARIMA 与随机森林的短期预测表现。
